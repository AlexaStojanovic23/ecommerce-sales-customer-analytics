# Key Insights

## Sales Channel Performance

Online was the strongest sales channel in June 2026, generating 7,310 in revenue from 7 orders.

The channel also had the highest average order value at approximately 1,044.29.

Marketplace generated 4,050 in revenue, while Store generated 3,190.

## City Performance

Belgrade generated the highest revenue with 5,380.

Nis followed with 4,020, while Kragujevac generated 3,230 and Novi Sad generated 1,920.

Although all cities had the same number of orders in the sample dataset, revenue differed significantly, suggesting differences in customer spending and product mix.

## Product Category Performance

Electronics was the dominant product category, generating 8,550 in revenue.

This was significantly higher than Clothing, Sports, and Home.

The Laptop was the highest-revenue product within Electronics, generating 5,000.

## Customer Performance

Marko Jovanovic was the highest-value customer in June 2026, generating 3,640 in revenue across two orders.

Stefan Markovic ranked second with 2,360 in revenue.

The customer segmentation analysis classified Marko Jovanovic as a VIP customer, while most customers were classified as Regular.

## Geographic Category Performance

Belgrade generated the highest Electronics revenue.

Nis performed best in Clothing, while Novi Sad generated the highest Home category revenue.

Sports revenue had a tie between Kragujevac and Novi Sad.

## Monthly Revenue Trend

Revenue in the sample dataset increased from 250 in May to 14,550 in June.

This represents a revenue increase of 14,300 compared with the previous month.

Because the sample dataset contains limited historical orders before June, this result should be interpreted as a demonstration of month-over-month SQL analysis rather than evidence of real seasonality or business growth.

## Analytical Takeaways

The analysis shows that revenue is highly concentrated in the Electronics category and that Online is the strongest sales channel in the sample dataset.

Customer-level analysis also shows that a small number of higher-value customers contribute a significant share of revenue.

Window functions were particularly useful for identifying top performers within groups, comparing orders with customer-level averages, and measuring revenue changes between months.
